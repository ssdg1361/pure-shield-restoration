## Why mold and water damage are so common in Boca Raton
Boca sits between the Atlantic and miles of Intracoastal and canal frontage, so humidity stays high year-round. That constant coastal moisture, plus salt air and homes that stay closed up and air-conditioned for months, gives mold a steady opening. The causes we see most often:

- **Coastal humidity and salt air.** Persistent moisture is the baseline here, and it shows up first in poorly ventilated closets, bathrooms, and laundry rooms.
- **Aging plumbing in older neighborhoods.** Homes around Old Floresta, Boca Villas, and the established streets near downtown often have original plumbing that develops slow leaks behind walls long before anyone notices.
- **AC condensate problems.** Air conditioners run hard in Boca's climate, and a clogged condensate line can quietly overflow into ceilings, closets, and walls.
- **Condo and HOA leaks.** In the buildings around Mizner Park, Royal Palm Place, and along the Intracoastal, a leak from the unit above is one of the most common ways water, and then mold, ends up in a home. We handle these regularly.
- **Storms and wind-driven rain.** Tropical systems and heavy rain push water in around windows, doors, and roofs, and even homes outside a flood zone can take on water.
